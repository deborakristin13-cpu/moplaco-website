Farm & processing

From seed to shipment.

Moplaco controls the entire chain — our own farm at Sheka, four washing stations in Sidamo and Yirgacheffe, an in-house quality lab, and export facilities in Addis Ababa and Dire Dawa.

Moplaco own farm · Kaffa Zone, Southwest Ethiopia
Sheka Forest Farm
A sanctuary of birds, animals, and trees — and Ethiopia's most extraordinary terroir

Sheka came into our life unexpectedly and after a long search. A sanctuary of birds, animals, and trees, this forest is ideal for coffee — and eco-tourism. We grow, process, and prepare our own coffee here alongside neighbouring farmers, using fully ecological practices: biogas cooking, composting, zero plastic, and fierce protection of the forest canopy. We also harvest honey, our bees pollinating and rejuvenating the forest around us.

Preservation
When deforestation ravages Ethiopia, our aim is to preserve every hectare of forest gifted to us — demonstrating that the forest is our greatest asset. Biogas, composting, zero plastic, shade canopy kept intact.
Coffee retreat
Sheka welcomes visitors who want to learn, relax, and participate in the community — farm walks, tastings, and direct connection to origin for roasters and enthusiasts alike.
Neighbours & crops
We support surrounding farmers with materials, training, and market access. We grow our own food so employees eat well — the community thrives alongside the forest.

Moplaco operates two export facilities — one in Addis Ababa handling all of Ethiopia's origins, and our founding facility in Dire Dawa, the historic gateway for Harar coffee since 1971.

Est. 1998 · 14,000 m²
Moplaco Addis Ababa

Our primary facility — 14,000 m² in the heart of Addis Ababa. Dry milling, grading, and export preparation for all origins. In-house Q grader lab on site. Handles Yirgacheffe, Sidamo, Guji, Limu, Sheka, and Jimma.

Est. 1971 · Founding facility
Moplaco Dire Dawa

Moplaco's original facility established in 1971 in Dire Dawa. Handles our legendary Star Brand Harar lots. The symbol of our founding mission — sharing Harar's Mocca flavour with the world since day one.

All lots are approved by certified Q graders at our Addis Ababa lab before export. Full moisture, water activity, and sensory grading sign-off. Pre-shipment samples available on request for all buyers. EUDR traceability documentation provided for European markets.

How cherry becomes green coffee
Harvest
Hand-picked ripe red cherry from Moplaco farms and partner farmers
Wet process
Pulping and fermentation (washed) or skin-on drying (natural / honey)
Drying
Raised African beds. Target 11–12% moisture. Water activity <0.65 Aw
Dry mill
Hulling, density sorting, colour grading at Addis or Dire Dawa
QC & cupping
Q grader approval — sensory, moisture, and water activity sign-off
Export
GrainPro bags, EUDR docs, Port of Djibouti to 40+ countries
Packaging
GrainPro-lined 60 kg jute bags. Hermetic seal maintains freshness throughout transit.
Shipping route
Via Port of Djibouti. ~25–35 days to Europe, ~35–45 days to Asia and the Americas.
Documentation
ICO certificate, phytosanitary, COO, organic cert, EUDR traceability data on request.
