Our story

Five decades of Ethiopian coffee — from Harar to the world

Born in the mountains of Harar-Hirna in 1940, our founder Yanni Georgalis grew up with coffee in his blood. Moplaco was established in 1971 in Dire Dawa and Harar — the historic heart of Harar coffee export — and has been growing ever since.

Today, under General Manager Heleanna Georgalis, Moplaco is a vertically integrated operation: we farm our own coffee at Sheka, operate washing stations across Sidamo and Yirgacheffe, run an in-house quality lab, and export to roasters across 40+ countries. We also roast and serve under our Galani Coffee brand in Addis Ababa.

Our name, Moplaco, stands for Mocca Plantation Company — a tribute to Harar coffee's legendary Mocca flavour. The three-pointed star in our logo is drawn from the Imperial symbol of Haile Selassie, representing the Power of the Trinity. Blue was chosen in memory of our Greek roots.

1971
Moplaco founded in Dire Dawa by Yanni Georgalis, exporting predominantly Harar coffee.
1990s – 2000s
Expansion into Addis Ababa. Chelelectu dry mill in Yirgacheffe acquired (2005), broadening origins to all of Ethiopia.
2010s
Focus shifts to specialty export. Sheka forest farm acquired. Boutique washing station in Sidamo Logita purchased (2017).
2018
Galani Coffee opens in Addis Ababa — Moplaco's consumer-facing café and roasting brand, bridging origin and cup.
2020
Moplaco named curator for Ethiopia's first Cup of Excellence — a milestone for Ethiopian specialty coffee on the world stage.
Today
Exporting to 40+ countries. 5 Galani cafés. EUDR-compliant. Farming, processing, exporting, and serving — from one family.

How we work

Exporter. Processor. Farmer.

Moplaco's unique strength is vertical integration from farm to freight — giving international buyers consistent quality they can trace at every step.

Farm
Our Sheka forest farm in southwest Ethiopia produces single-origin lots under direct management. Neighbouring farmers supply cherry to our stations.
Process
Washing stations in Sidamo Logita, Yirgacheffe, Chelelectu, and Chelbesa. Both washed and natural process. Every lot traceable to station and kebele.
Quality control
In-house lab with certified Q graders. Moisture, water activity (target <0.65 Aw), and full sensory cupping at intake, pre-shipment, and export.
Export
GrainPro-lined bags, EUDR-compliant documentation, coordination via Port of Djibouti. Export to Europe, Asia, North America, and the Middle East.

Sustainability & social impact

Good coffee means good communities

Moplaco works directly with farming communities surrounding our washing stations in Sidamo, Yirgacheffe, and Sheka. We invest in school repairs, classroom furniture, and farmer support programmes.

We actively advocate against eucalyptus monoculture — which depletes soil fertility in coffee regions — and promote shade-grown, organically managed farming that sustains both the land and the livelihoods connected to it.

EUDR-ready: Moplaco's supply chain documentation meets the EU Deforestation Regulation requirements, giving buyers in Europe the traceability assurance they need from 2025 onwards.
